Purpose:
  Calibrate thermistors & INA228 boards in a temperature controlled bath.

Equipment:
    Microcontroller (mcu):
      Wemos Lolin ESP32-S3

    INA228 power monitors, 4x:
      Adafruit INA228 (ADA5832).
      I2C 85V, 20-bit High or Low Side Power Monitor featuring Texas Instruments
      INA228.
      NOTE: Replaced the onboard shunt resistor of 0.015 Ohm with a 1.0 Ohm one.

    Thermistors, 4x:
      Measurement Specialties (GA)G22K7MCD419.
      Glass bead Ø0.38mm, 30 ms response time in liquids.

    Temperature bath:
      PolyScience PD15R-30-A12E.
      15 mK temperature stability.

    PT100 probe:
      Picotech PT-104.
      Accuracy: 0.015 K
      Resolution: 0.001 K

Each thermistor is connected to a single dedicated INA228 board in high-side
power configuration, meaning:
  V_bus & Vin+ are connected together to the 3.3V output of the mcu.
  Vin- is connected to one thermistor lead.
  Other thermistor lead is connected to the GND of the mcu.

We specifically keep each thermistor matched to a particular INA228 board. We
calibrate resistance to temperature by placing all thermistors inside the
temperature controlled bath, and calibrating their resistances against an
additional PT100 temperature probe (Picotech PT-104).

The bath temperature is set to a quasi-stationary ramps of 1 K / 10 min.

Findings after plotting results:
  Turns out that the down ramps have an offset with respect to the up ramps of
  about 0.2 K. This is probably due to the fact that the tip of the PT104 probe
  was inside a blind hole of a large copper body, intended to smoothen out
  temperature fluctuations inside of the temperature bath. The thermistors
  themselves were exposed directly to the water. This caused a thermal inertia
  of the PT104 probe with respect to the thermistors, causing the offset.

  Solution:
  Redo the calibration, but now with the tip of PT104 probe directly exposed to
  the bath water, with the thermistors right next to the probe tip.
